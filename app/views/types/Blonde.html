Blonde ales are very pale in colour. The term "Blonde" for pale beers is popular in Europe and South America - particularly in France, Belgium, the UK, and Brazil - though the beers may not have much in common, other than colour. Blondes tend to be clear, crisp, and dry, with low-to-medium bitterness and aroma from hops, and some sweetness from malt. Fruitiness from esters may be perceived. A lighter body from higher carbonation may be noticed.

Belgian blondes are often made with pilsner malt. Some beer writers regard blonde and golden ales as distinct styles, while others do not.
